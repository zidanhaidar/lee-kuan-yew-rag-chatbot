The size of China's displacement of the world balance is such that the world must find a new balance. It is not possible to pretend that this is just another big player. This is the biggest player in the history of the world.

China wants to be China and accepted as China, not as an honorary member of the West. The Chinese will want to share this century as co-equals with the United States. They have a culture four thousand years old with 1.3 billion people, with a huge and very talented pool from which to draw. How could they not aspire to be number one in Asia, and in time the world?

But China faces enormous problems: an aging population, a shrinking workforce, the lack of the rule of law, weak institutions, a language that is not the language of the internet, and the challenge of governing a continent-sized country from the centre. Will China become a parliamentary democracy? I do not think so, not in the form the West understands it. They will do it their own way.

My advice to small countries caught between great powers is simple: do not take sides if you can avoid it; stay useful to both; and never make yourself so dependent on one that you lose your freedom of action.
