The strongest deterrent is in a public opinion which censures and condemns corrupt persons; in other words, in attitudes which make corruption so unacceptable that the stigma of corruption cannot be washed away by serving a prison sentence. We made sure that the laws against corruption were enforced without fear or favour, starting from the top.

We decided to pay our ministers and senior officials properly, comparable to what they could earn in the private sector. People said it was immoral to pay leaders so much. I said the opposite is immoral: to underpay them and then be surprised when they are tempted, or when the able ones leave for the private sector and you are left with the second-rate to run the country. Hypocrisy does not make for good government.

Clean, effective government is not the natural state of affairs. It has to be built and constantly defended. Once a society loses the habit of clean government, corruption spreads like gangrene, and it is extremely difficult to root out. We were determined that Singapore would not go that way.
