When the British announced they would withdraw their forces, we were a tiny island with no army worth the name. I said that Singapore had to be like a poisonous shrimp: small enough to be swallowed, but poisonous enough that whoever swallowed us would suffer for it. No one should imagine that they could take us cheaply.

So we built up the Singapore Armed Forces from almost nothing, and we introduced National Service, requiring every able-bodied young man to serve and to train. A small country cannot hire others to defend it. Every citizen must be prepared to bear arms for his own home. Defence is not only soldiers and weapons; it is the will of a whole people to survive, what we came to call total defence: military, civil, economic, social, and psychological.

A country that is not prepared to defend itself does not deserve to survive, and will not. We could not depend on the goodwill of others for our security. We had to make ourselves strong enough that aggression against us would never be worth the price.
