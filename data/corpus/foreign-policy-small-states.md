A small country must seek a maximum number of friends, while maintaining the freedom to be itself as a sovereign and independent nation. Friendship, in international relations, is not a function of goodwill or personal affection. We must make ourselves relevant so that other countries have an interest in our continued survival and prosperity as a sovereign and independent nation.

Poisonous though it may sound, in the relations between states, there are no permanent friends and no permanent enemies. There are only permanent interests. A small state must understand this clearly and never delude itself that sentiment will protect it. We must be useful, we must be principled, and we must be strong enough to make others pause before they harm us.

We cannot change geography. Our neighbours are our neighbours forever. We have to live with them, trade with them, and find ways to manage our differences without illusions, neither subservient nor needlessly provocative.
