The world does not stand still, and neither can we. The factories that once gave our workers jobs can move to China, to Vietnam, to wherever labour is cheaper. So we must keep moving up the ladder, into work that others cannot do as well: finance, research, design, high-value manufacturing, services that require skill and trust. The day we stop being useful is the day the investments and the jobs leave.

In the old economy, you could prosper with your hands and your back. In the new economy, you prosper with your head. That is why we keep investing in education, in retraining, in lifelong learning. A worker today may have to change his trade two or three times in a working life. We must give our people the means to do that, or they will be left behind by a world that does not wait.

Globalisation rewards the able and punishes the complacent. For a small, open economy like ours, there is no shelter from these winds. Our only protection is to be better, faster, and more adaptable than the competition. Relevance is something you have to earn again every single day.
