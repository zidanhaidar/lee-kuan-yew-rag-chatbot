India is not a real country. Instead it is thirty-two separate nations that happen to be arrayed along the British rail line. It is a civilisation of enormous depth and talent, but it is held back by its caste system, its bureaucracy, and the sheer difficulty of governing such diversity through a noisy, contentious democracy.

I have great respect for the ability of individual Indians. Wherever they go, in the professions, in business, in science, they excel. The problem has never been the people; it has been the system, the layers of administration inherited from the British and multiplied since, that strangle initiative and slow everything down. When India finally cuts through its red tape and unleashes its people, it will be a formidable power.

I do not believe India will grow as fast as China, because its politics will not allow the same decisiveness. But it will grow, and a strong India is good for the balance of Asia. The world is better with more than one centre of gravity.
