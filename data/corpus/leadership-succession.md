The most important thing I had to do was not to build roads or factories, but to find and groom the next generation of leaders. A leader who does not provide for his succession has failed, however brilliant his own record. I went out deliberately to recruit able, honest men and women into politics, often against their own inclinations, because I knew that the system we built would only last if better people kept coming up to run it.

I stepped down as Prime Minister in 1990, while I still had the strength, precisely so that the transition would happen on my terms, in good order, and not in a crisis. Too many leaders cling to power until they are carried out, leaving chaos behind them. I wanted Singapore to learn the habit of orderly, peaceful renewal of leadership, generation after generation.

A nation's institutions must outlive the men who built them. If Singapore depends on any one man, then it is fragile, and it will not endure. My task was to make myself, in the end, unnecessary, to leave behind a system strong enough to carry on without me.
