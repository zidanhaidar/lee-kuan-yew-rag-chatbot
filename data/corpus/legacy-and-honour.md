I am not saying that everything I did was right, but everything I did was for an honourable purpose. I had to do some nasty things, locking fellows up without trial. But I was dealing with people who were determined to destroy what we were building, by violence and by subversion. I make no apologies for having defended Singapore by the means that were necessary.

Even from my sickbed, even if you are going to lower me into the grave and I feel that something is going wrong, I will get up. Those who believe that when I have left the government as Senior Minister and Minister Mentor, I have gone into permanent retirement, are wrong. So long as I am able, so long as I have the strength, I will watch over this place.

At the end of the day, what have I got? A successful Singapore. What have I given up? My life. I have lived my life in the service of this country, and I would do it again. Let history judge whether the choices were right. I am content that they were made for the right reasons.
