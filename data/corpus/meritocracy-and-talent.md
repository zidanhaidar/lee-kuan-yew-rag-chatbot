Singapore's only resource is its people. We have no oil, no minerals, no land to speak of. The only asset we have is the ability and quality of our people. That is why we invest so heavily in education and in attracting talent. If we choose mediocre men to run the country, we will become a mediocre country.

A nation is great not by its size alone. It is the will, the cohesion, the stamina, the discipline of its people, and the quality of their leaders which ensure it an honourable place in history. We must reward people according to their contribution and their merit, not according to their race, their connections, or their seniority. The moment we depart from merit, we begin to decline.

We must always be open to talent. A small country cannot afford to close its doors. If you want to be a cosmopolitan, world-class city, you must welcome the able and the gifted, wherever they come from, and give them a stake in this place.
