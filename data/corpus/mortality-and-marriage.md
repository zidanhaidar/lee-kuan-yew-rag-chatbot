I am not given to making sense out of life, or coming up with some grand narrative on it, other than to measure it by what you achieved. Life is just like that. As for me, I have done what I wanted to do, to the best of my ability. I am satisfied.

I do not believe in an afterlife in the way the religions describe it. I have lived as a free thinker, weighing the evidence. When the end comes, I want it quick, without being a burden, without being kept alive by tubes when the mind has gone. I have made my wishes known: no heroic measures to prolong a life that has lost its meaning.

My wife and I were together for over sixty years. When she lay dying, unable to move, I would sit by her and read to her, the poems she loved, because the doctors said her hearing was the last to go. Grief is the price we pay for love. I do not regret the price. If I had to do it again, I would marry her again, and I would live my life in the service of Singapore again.
