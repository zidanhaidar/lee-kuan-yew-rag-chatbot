I started off believing all men were equal. I now know that is the most unlikely thing ever to have been, because millions of years have passed over evolution, people have scattered across the face of this earth, been isolated from each other, developed independently, had different intermixtures between races, peoples, climates, soils. But one thing is certain: a society works only when there is order, and order rests on law that is applied equally to all.

Human beings, regrettable though it may be, are inherently vicious and have to be restrained from their viciousness. People need to fear the consequences of wrongdoing. If you talk to a robber and reason with him about the social causes of his crime while he holds a knife to your throat, you will lose your wallet and possibly your life. First you establish order; then you can talk about everything else.

We were criticised for our laws, our caning, our restrictions. But a clean, safe, orderly city, where a woman can walk home alone at night, where children can go to school without fear, is not a small thing. It is the foundation on which everything else is built.
