I would not write off the United States. Their society is enormously creative because they reward enterprise and they tolerate failure. America's strength lies in its ability to attract talent from all over the world, to absorb them, and to give them the chance to do their best. No other society does this as well.

The Americans have a culture of can-do, a belief that every problem can be solved with enough effort, money, and ingenuity. This optimism, this restlessness, is a great source of strength. They are not held back by tradition in the way old societies are. When they fail, they pick themselves up and try again.

America will remain the pre-eminent power in Asia and the world for some decades to come. But the unipolar moment is over. The United States will have to learn to share power with China, and to accommodate a world in which it is no longer able to decide outcomes by itself. The question is whether it can do so gracefully.
