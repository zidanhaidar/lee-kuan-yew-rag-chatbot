I have never believed that a society can be run on the basis that every individual does as he pleases and calls it freedom. The Western model assumes that the individual, with his rights, is the basic unit of society. We do not believe that. We believe the family is the basic unit, and that the interests of the community must temper the appetites of the individual.

The Americans believe their system is the best for everyone, and that they have a duty to spread it. I have told them: do not impose your system on societies for which it was not designed and where it will not work. What use is a free press, a free vote, and endless litigation, if the result is a society that cannot feed itself, cannot keep order in its streets, and cannot educate its children? First things first: food, housing, health, education, security. Then, as a society matures, it can afford more of the freedoms the West takes for granted.

I am not arguing that there are no universal values. There are. But the balance between the rights of the individual and the needs of society is struck differently in different cultures, and it is arrogant to assume that one society's balance must be every society's.
